PI Planning Preparation – Regional Bureau
1. Objectives
The primary objective of this PI planning session is to coordinate and align work across the CGI in support of CGFS and the regional executive offices, in line with the MOUs for:
WHA (Western Hemisphere Affairs)
SCA (South and Central Asia)
CARE
NEA (Near Eastern Affairs)
Iraq portfolio
Iran / Middle East Evacuations
The session aims to:
Gain a clear understanding of ongoing initiatives, statutory and policy driven responsibilities, and priorities for each regional bureau and portfolio.
Establish a path forward for each bureau, including next steps, key deliverables, and timelines, documented through bureau specific Strategic Roadmaps.
Define crosscutting workstreams (e.g., appropriations support, budget execution support, training/OCM, environments and integrations, licensing/funding, and risk/dispute management) to meet MOU obligations.
Outputs of the PI planning session will include:
Draft Strategic Roadmaps for WHA, NEA/SCAEX (including Iraq), and CARE, to be reviewed and approved by bureau leadership and CGFS/CIO in subsequent quarterly roadmap reviews.
A prioritized PI backlog of epics and features that aligns with the MOUs and the government fiscal year.

As opportunities arise and funding is made available, additional regional partners and enterprise capabilities will be incorporated:
Regional Bureaus: Ukraine, EUR, EAP, AF.
Regional Portal: Central hub for all regional tools, solutions, and documentation (including disaster readiness and representation/gift tracking).
Overseas Dashboard: Aggregates data from posts worldwide and supports overseas budget execution, traceability, and foreign assistance oversight.
2.3 Governance and Roadmap Cadence
In alignment with the MOUs:
Each regional partner (WHA, NEA/SCAEX, CARE, Iraq) will maintain a Strategic Roadmap that:
Includes high level milestones and objectives decomposed into epics and features.
Is reviewed and refined during quarterly roadmap review sessions with CGFS/CIO and partner bureau leadership.
PI Planning will:
Update the Strategic Roadmaps.
Identify PI level objectives and features aligned to those roadmaps.
Prepare for backlog grooming before each PI and quarterly roadmap review.
3. Vision
The long-term vision for CGI’s work in the regional bureaus is to continue expanding CGI’s presence and support across additional regional sections while standardizing core financial solutions and sharing best practices across the enterprise.
Currently, CGI supports WHA, SCA, and NEA (including CARE and Iraq). The strategic growth plan includes:
EAP: Targeted for procurement in the current fiscal year Q4 – Q1 FY27.
Ukraine and AF: Targeted for procurement by FY27.
EUR: Targeted for procurement by FY28.
Key Outcomes Sought
Distribution of the Domestic Budget Execution and Tracking Solution To current regional partners (WHA, SCA/NEA), including:
Budget Formulation Module: Streamlining and standardizing domestic budget planning and submission.
Domestic Dashboard Module: Providing actionable insights and data visualization (plan vs execution, burn rate, projections, Status of Funds) for leadership and program users.
Development of Overseas Solutions
Overseas Budget Formulation Solution: Enabling budget planning and execution tracking at post level.
Overseas Dashboard:
Visualizing posts on a world map.
Enabling bureaus to understand missions’ locations, timing, scope, and financial posture globally.
Supporting overseas traceability and foreign assistance oversight.
Implementation of the Regional Portal
Central hub for all regional tools, solutions, documentation, and training.
Seamless access and integration to domestic, overseas, disaster readiness, and representation/gift capabilities.
Disaster Readiness Workstream
A module within the portal that:
Provides tools and structured data entry for global users to record and track disaster related spending and activities.
Includes SOPs and quick reference guidance for what to do when an event occurs (functional playbooks aligned with bureau policies).
Supports leadership reporting and comprehensive disaster data perspectives.
Representation and Gift Application
Domestic and overseas data capture for representation and gift events.
Rollup into bureau and regional dashboards for spend tracking and compliance.
Support for Appropriations Cycle and Oversight (NEA/SCAEX and CARE)
Appropriations Cycle Roadmaps and SOPs. (Didn’t need it so we didn’t do it)
Annual Spend Plan support.
BRR/CBJ/OMB/Congressional support.
Historical crosswalks for CARE (OAW/CARE/EW).
4. Opportunities and Challenges
4.1 Opportunities
Expansion of regional partnerships to Ukraine, EUR, AF, and EAP.
Extension of domestic and overseas solutions to post level operations.
Proactive technology integration: leveraging AI tools (e.g., StateChat, GitHub Copilot) and modern low code platforms to enhance efficiency and user experience.
Disaster readiness solution: a flexible module supporting global users, leadership views, and compliance with audit/oversight expectations.
Regional collaboration and knowledge sharing: using common frameworks and the Regional Portal to reduce duplication and accelerate adoption.
Grants & DRL PRM
4.2 Challenges
Resource allocation and continuity: staff shifting between projects disrupts knowledge transfer and can impact roadmap commitments.
Siloed solutioning: limited visibility across regional projects leads to duplication and missed synergies; the Regional Portal and quarterly roadmap reviews will mitigate this.
Security and compliance risks: overseas dashboards and integrations must adhere to least privilege access, CGFS/DT policies, and audit readiness requirements.
Cost effective disaster readiness: delivering robust, scalable solutions that remain within bureau funding constraints.
Transition from SAP Hana to Data Bricks (Data lake): Started to implement some of the data SOF is coming from datalake.
Licensing and Funding: Clarifying who funds which licenses and environments for each bureau; aligning epics to quarterly funding reviews and MOU mandated cost estimation processes.
Managing urgent/emergency work (e.g., Middle East evacuations) without jeopardizing core roadmap commitments.
5. Prioritization Approach
5.1 Urgent/Emergency Work
Once a baseline PI plan is established, urgent or emergency work (e.g., crisis events, new Congressional reporting) will be prioritized and accepted as needed.
Sprint schedules and PI scope will be adjusted through formal change control and communication with partner bureau leadership.
For urgent work, we will document impacts to scope, schedule, and funding, in line with MOU requirements for cost estimation and approvals.
5.2 Strategic Focus
Deliver the WHA Domestic Budget Execution & Tracking solution first, as a template for NEA, SCA, and others, to streamline subsequent implementations.
Implement crosscutting workstreams that support all regional partners, including:
Appropriations and Spend Plan support (NEA/SCAEX & CARE).
Budget Execution & Operations Support.
Requirements Documents, training, documentation, and change management.
Environments and data integrations.
Licensing & Cost Management.
6. Success Criteria
Success at the end of the planning year will be measured by:
Stakeholder Approval
Signed off Strategic Roadmaps and requirements/design documentation by all relevant stakeholders (CGFS/CIO, partner bureaus).
Formal software acceptance (post UAT) for key releases.
Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Reduction in time managing untagged or misclassified transactions.
Improved data visibility (e.g., adoption of dashboards, reduction in manual reports).
Decreased time to estimate future year budgets and generate Spend Plan and appropriations materials.
Number of active users and frequency of key actions in the domestic and overseas solutions.
Audit readiness ($ focus)
Recovery Funds (Getting ULOs back)
Admin Standup time
Error reduction
User Acceptance Testing (UAT)
Completion of planned UAT cycles (e.g., at least one two week UAT period per major release), with issues triaged and remediated.
Documentation and Training
Delivery of user guides, quick reference guides (QRGs), SOPs, and technical documentation for all in scope solutions.
Completion of agreed training sessions and briefings with bureau stakeholders; positive feedback on usability and clarity.
